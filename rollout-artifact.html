Program Rollout Roadmap

A four-view rollout package built for a multi-location pricing and program launch, covering the executive roadmap, week-by-week support timeline, field communication plan, and launch support structure. Designed to sequence enablement so every audience, central team, regional leadership, and location owners, knows exactly what to expect and when.

Rollout Planning Stakeholder Comms Change Management RACI Design Field Enablement Multi-Audience Communication Program Management

Overview

Role

Program Manager, planning and communications lead

Type

Rollout roadmap, communication timeline, and support structure

Scope

Multi-location service network, new pricing and program launch

Duration

6-week rollout, plus a post-launch feedback and metrics window

The Problem

A new pricing and program structure had to reach dozens of locations at once, with a central team, regional leadership, and location owners all needing the right information at the right time. Without a clear sequence, launch-day support gets overwhelmed and locations feel unprepared.

What I Did

I built a four-view rollout package: an executive roadmap, a week-by-week support timeline, a field communication plan, and a launch support structure. Each milestone names the audience, the purpose, and the review agenda, so every group knows what to expect and when.

How It Works

  • Weeks 1, 2, Foundation: Organize the Launch Support Team and deliver leadership education.
  • Weeks 3, 4, Briefing & Training: Educate the central team, review FAQs, complete full training.
  • Weeks 5, 6, Go Live: Run scheduled support calls, release the program, and provide live field assistance.
  • Weeks 8, 9, Follow-Up: Survey regional leadership and analyze success and adoption metrics on a 30-60-90 cadence.

Why It Matters

The plan front-loads enablement so support is proactive rather than reactive, and it closes with a metrics-driven feedback loop to improve the next rollout. It shows how I turn a complex, multi-audience launch into a clear, executable schedule with built-in measurement.

At a Glance

Program Manager owns the plan, the enablement, and the feedback loop.
Central Team owns education and go-live readiness.
Launch Support Team carries the field-facing calls.
Regional Leadership and Location Owners own decisions inside their own scope, staffing and readiness.

What Worked & What I Would Change

Front-loading enablement paid off.

By briefing the Central Team and standing up a dedicated Launch Support Team before any location-facing call, launch-day support was proactive instead of reactive. The people answering questions already knew the answers.

Naming an audience and purpose for every milestone reduced confusion.

Each step in the timeline states who is invited and what the call covers. That single habit cut the "wait, is this for me?" back-and-forth that usually slows a multi-audience rollout.

One accountable owner per activity kept decisions moving.

The RACI made it clear that Regional Leadership owned readiness and Location Owners owned staffing, so those calls did not stall waiting on the central team.

What I would change next time.

I would add a mid-rollout checkpoint between the readiness review and go live to catch gaps earlier, and I would define the post-launch survey questions up front so the feedback loop starts on day one rather than two weeks later.
